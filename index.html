Challenge

Managing HR across a 3,000-employee banking institution requires precision at every step — from payroll compliance to leave management and workforce reporting. For NDB Bank, the growing complexity of their HR operations had outpaced the tools they relied on.

Manual spreadsheets, disconnected attendance systems, and a month-end payroll cycle that consumed two full weeks of HR team time were creating risk, not managing it. A single payroll error in a regulated financial institution is not just an inconvenience — it triggers audit queries, erodes employee trust, and creates compliance exposure. NDB Bank's HR leadership recognised they needed a platform built for this level of accountability.

Legacy Systems

NDB Bank's HR operations were spread across a patchwork of systems: attendance captured via biometric hardware that didn't sync automatically, leave applications managed through email chains, and payroll calculated in spreadsheets that required manual reconciliation with multiple statutory deduction tables.

The disconnect between systems meant that by the time data reached payroll, it had been touched — and potentially altered — by multiple hands. Audit trails were incomplete. Variance reports were unreliable. And the HR team, despite their diligence, spent more time fixing data than acting on it.

As NDB Bank expanded its branch network and headcount grew, the cracks widened. The same process that took a week in 2020 now consumed three. Onboarding a new branch cohort required manual data entry across four separate systems. The cost of doing nothing was no longer hidden.

PeoplesHR Solution

NDB Bank selected PeoplesHR as their unified HR platform following a structured evaluation that assessed compliance readiness, integration capability with existing biometric infrastructure, and the depth of Sri Lankan statutory payroll support.

The implementation covered four core areas:

  • Payroll & Compliance: Automated EPF, ETF, and PAYE calculations with built-in audit trails, eliminating the manual reconciliation process and reducing month-end close from 14 days to 4.
  • Attendance & Shift Management: Direct integration with NDB Bank's biometric hardware, bringing real-time attendance visibility across all branches with automated exception flagging.
  • Leave Management: Policy-driven leave rules, multi-level digital approvals, and accrual automation replaced the email-based workflow entirely.
  • Workforce Analytics: HR leadership gained access to live headcount, attrition, and cost dashboards — enabling data-driven decisions for the first time.

The rollout was completed across all branches in under 90 days, supported by PeoplesHR's dedicated implementation and training team.

Results

Since go-live, NDB Bank has recorded zero payroll errors. The month-end payroll cycle, previously a two-week ordeal, now closes in four days. HR processing time across leave, attendance, and reporting has fallen by 70%.

The shift from reactive administration to proactive workforce management has been the most significant change. HR leadership can now pull a live attrition report, model the cost impact of a shift adjustment, or generate a statutory compliance pack in minutes rather than days.

"PeoplesHR transformed the way our HR team operates. We went from spending most of our time fixing data to actually using it. The payroll compliance module alone gave us confidence we'd never had before."

Head of Human Resources, NDB Bank

Employee satisfaction with HR services has also improved measurably. Self-service leave applications, digital payslips, and mobile access to HR data have reduced HR helpdesk queries by over 60%.

Next Steps

NDB Bank is now exploring Lexi — PeoplesHR's AI-powered HR copilot — to surface predictive attrition signals and automate workforce planning recommendations ahead of their next strategic review cycle.

The bank is also working with PeoplesHR to extend the platform to their subsidiary entities, bringing the same compliance rigour and process efficiency to a broader group workforce.
